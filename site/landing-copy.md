## 02 — how it works

**it reads, it drafts, it's in.**

- **see** — summon blink on any thread, doc, or dm. it reads what's on your screen. no pasting, no explaining.
- **draft** — it writes the reply the way you would: your phrasing, your tone, the context already there.
- **insert** — the draft lands straight in the box you were typing in. read it, tweak it, send.

## 03 — why blink

**_it feels like blink is reading my mind._** (first-person, italic — the felt impression, not a sourced quote)

here's why:

- **it sees what you see.** the context is already on your screen, so you stop re-explaining yourself to a chatbot.
- **it sounds like you, not like an ai.** drafts in your voice instead of generic filler.

## 04 — your screen (privacy)

**reading your screen, without the creep.**

blink reads your screen so you never have to re-explain the conversation. here's what that does and doesn't mean.

- **it only looks when you summon it.** the rest of the time blink is idle. no background watching, no always-on recording.
- **only what you point it at.** when you ask for a draft, blink reads the screen in front of you and uses ai to write the reply. just that screen, just when you asked.
- **you stay in control, and you can check.** blink writes a draft and stops — you decide what to send. and because it's source-available, you can read exactly what it captures, or run the whole thing yourself.

> Open consistency note: privacy says "just that screen" but the scrollback FAQ
> says it reads beyond what's visible. Reconcile to "just the conversation in
> front of you, just when you asked" before shipping.

## 05 — faq

**questions people actually ask.**

- **who is blink for?** founders living in claude code, their inbox, and x at the same time. creators and influencers buried in dms and replies. if you want to stop context switching, blink's for you.
- **does blink send messages for me?** no. it writes the draft into the box. you read it, edit it, and hit send yourself.
- **what apps does it work in?** anywhere you type — imessage, slack, gmail, discord, docs, and most native and web apps.
- **can blink see the whole conversation, or just what's on screen?** the whole conversation. it doesn't just grab the last few messages you can see — it reads the full thread, so the draft actually has the context.
- **what happens to what blink reads?** to write a draft, the text on your screen goes to an ai model. some of it is kept to help us improve blink — you can turn that off anytime. and if you'd rather keep everything on your own machine, blink is source-available to self-host.
- **what will it cost?** blink will be a paid product. the beta is hands-on and free while we get it right — we'll talk pricing with you directly before it ever costs you anything.
- **what do i need to run it?** a mac, a couple of permissions (accessibility and screen recording), and a hotkey to summon blink. takes about a minute to set up.
- **windows or iphone?** not yet. blink is macOS only for now — join the waitlist and we'll let you know if that changes.
- **why is it invite-only?** because we onboard every person ourselves — set you up, learn how you work, and tune blink to fit. that only works a few people at a time, so we invite in small batches. leave your email and we'll reach out when there's room.

## Confirmed product facts behind the copy (must stay true)

- Reads the screen **only on summon** (hotkey), no background capture.
- **Inserts** the draft directly into the active field (NOT a ⌘V paste). User edits + sends.
- Reads the **full conversation**, including content scrolled out of view.
- Screen text goes to an **AI model (Gemini)**; some is **retained to improve the product**, with a **user opt-out toggle**. Do NOT claim "local-first" or "nothing stored."
- **Self-host** (source-available, ELv2) is the full-privacy path.
- Needs **Accessibility + Screen Recording** permissions + a hotkey.
- **Invite-only = white-glove** individual onboarding (deliberate, not just scarcity).
- **Pricing is not public** — paid product, priced 1:1 during onboarding.
- Beachhead audience: **founders (Claude Code + email + X) and creators/influencers**; support/sales/recruiting are later.
